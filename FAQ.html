Frequently Asked Questions

Organic farming is an agricultural method that avoids the use of synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, and genetically modified organisms. Instead, it emphasizes natural processes, biodiversity, and sustainability.

Organic products are free from harmful chemicals and pesticides. They are better for the environment, promote biodiversity, and are often fresher and tastier compared to conventionally grown products.

To start organic farming, you need to familiarize yourself with organic farming principles, select appropriate crops, and prepare your soil. It’s also beneficial to seek guidance from organic farming experts and organizations.

Organic farming benefits include improved soil health, reduced environmental impact, better biodiversity, and healthier food. It also supports sustainable agricultural practices and reduces exposure to harmful chemicals.
